Cross-linguistic semantic representation in the human hippocampus

Yan, X., Sheth, S. A., & Hayden, B. Y.

Under review

Using rare intracranial recordings from bilingual epilepsy patients, we discovered that individual hippocampal neurons maintain shared semantic geometry across English and Spanish while using different neural coordinate systems. This suggests the brain preserves meaning relationships across languages through distributed population codes rather than sparse "concept cells."

Distinct computational mechanisms of uncertainty processing explain opposing exploratory behaviors in anxiety and apathy

Yan, X., Ebitz, R. B., Grissom, N., Darrow, D. P., & Herman, A. B.

Biological Psychiatry: Cognitive Neuroscience and Neuroimaging, 2025

We identified distinct computational mechanisms underlying exploration deficits in anxiety versus apathy. Anxiety increases sensitivity to environmental uncertainty, driving excessive exploration; apathy reduces sensitivity to reward information, causing exploitation of known options. These opposing patterns emerge from separable cognitive computations.
