About Me

I am a researcher in human neuroscience, with a deep passion for understanding the cognitive and neural mechanisms behind depression and anxiety. Although this may seem like a monumental challenge for a single lifetime, I have dedicated myself to making meaningful strides since beginning my research journey in 2016

In the early years of my career, I focused on human social behaviors and adaptation, investigating how oxytocin and its placebo effects could foster prosocial behaviors and improve social well-being. This work sparked my interest in the powerful role that neural and psychological mechanisms play in shaping human behavior and mental health

Since 2022, I have turned my attention to the computational underpinnings of mental illness, particularly depression and anxiety. Understanding how brain dysfunction contributes to these conditions is key to advancing neuro-modulation techniques, with the potential to treat mental illness at its source

Recognizing the critical importance of early intervention, I co-founded NAODAO in 2020 to develop systems that track mental health and facilitate early diagnosis, with the ultimate goal of preventing mental illness from escalating
