Reviewer Comments & Responses

Transparency in the peer review process - sharing critiques and how they improved my work.

Paper: Nonlinear modulation of human exploration (bioRxiv, 2025)
Reviewer 1: "The computational model seems overly complex for the behavioral data presented. The authors should justify why simpler models were not sufficient."
Our Response: We added a comprehensive model comparison section, testing 8 simpler models. We demonstrated through cross-validation and parameter recovery that the complex model was necessary to capture the nonlinear dynamics in the data. This strengthened our methodology section significantly.
Paper: Distinct computational mechanisms in anxiety and apathy (Biological Psychiatry, 2025)
Reviewer 2: "The clinical implications are unclear. How would these findings translate to treatment?"
Our Response: We added a new section on translational implications, including specific computational targets for intervention. We also initiated a collaboration with clinicians to test these predictions, which is now mentioned in the discussion.
Paper: Neural dynamics of self-referential processing (J Neuroscience, 2024)
Reviewer 3: "The sample size (n=20) is concerning for making broad claims about neural mechanisms."
Our Response: We conducted a thorough power analysis showing our within-subject design had adequate power (>0.8) for our main effects. We also toned down our claims and added limitations section acknowledging generalizability concerns. Additionally, we pre-registered a replication study with n=40.
Grant Review: NIH K99/R00 Application
Study Section Comment: "The candidate shows promise but lacks sufficient preliminary data for the proposed single-neuron recordings."
My Plan: Currently collecting pilot data with 5 patients. Will resubmit with preliminary findings showing feasibility and initial results. Also expanding collaboration network with established single-unit recording labs.
Conference Abstract: Computational Psychiatry 2023
Review Committee: "The theoretical framework needs clearer connection to existing computational psychiatry models."
Revision: Rewrote the abstract to explicitly link our uncertainty framework to established models (e.g., predictive coding, active inference). The revised version was accepted for poster presentation.

Note: These critiques, though initially difficult to receive, ultimately strengthened my work and taught me the value of rigorous peer review.
